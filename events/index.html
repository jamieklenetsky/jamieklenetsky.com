C4: The Choral Composer/Conductor Collective - "This Fugitive Soul"

Arthur Rimbaud: This Fugitive Soul
June 14 and 15, 2024
Manhattan

The poet Arthur Rimbaud burst upon the Paris literary scene at the age of 17, making a name for himself with his shocking, visionary poetry, and his equally shocking lifestyle. But he turned his back on poetry completely just a few years later. He traveled the world, and then lived in Ethiopia for several years before his early death at 37. During his time in remote obscurity, his reputation grew to the point that there were calls for a statue to be erected in his honor. But despite many rumors of his whereabouts, the first confirmed sighting was at his funeral in his hometown on the Belgian border.

C4 presents an original semi-staged work about the life of Rimbaud. Featuring new music by C4 composers, and centered around Steven Gerber’s 20-minute epic Une Saison en Enfer based on Rimbaud’s prose poem of the same name, the work will delve into how Rimbaud’s strange life unfolded as a working-out of his unique vision of himself and his place in the world. The text for the work is taken not only from Rimbaud’s writings, but also quotes from those who knew him.

A concert featuring the music of Tudor composer Christopher Tye. C4: The Composer/Conductor Collective - "Connections"

Connections
St. Joseph's Church, Manhattan, November 14, 2019
Church of Saint Luke in the Fields, Manhattan, November 16, 2019

In "Connections," the first cycle of C4's 15th season, C4 explores what it means to connect to others through music, shared experience, technology, and more. Joined by the piano/violin duo aTonalHits, C4 will premiere the duo's piece "Three for Four," with text inspired by iPhone autotranslation misunderstandings. aTonalHits will also join C4 for Reena Esmail's "When the violin" and the world premiere of Perry Townsend's "Stock Purchase Agreement." Also on the program: works by Robert Cohen, Natalie Dietterich, Ko Matsushita, David See, and Caroline Shaw.
